A few more questions to calculate your liablity rating

Incorporating limits the exposure to personal liability of the owners. Without incorporating, business owners can be personally responsible for business liabilities, including debts and legal judgments.

Do you hold onto your client's property?

Client property can include physical property and money. Some professions require that property be held for clients for a certain time period, such as for deposits, repair of property, and other cases.

Do you hold sensitive client information?

Sensitive client information can include your client's banking details, health information, personally identifiable information and financial information.

Do you operate on the property of others?

When you conduct work for your clients, do you operate on their property? If so, check 'Yes'.

Do you rely on sub-contractors?

Sub-Contractors include anyone that you may hire to assist work that you do for a client. This does not include employees.
